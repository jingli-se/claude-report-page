Experiment Execution Deviation: The client paused the control group (Campaign 65429) on March 23rd, violating the parallel-run requirement of the experiment design. This creates unequal flight durations across groups. Metrics in this report reflect the matched observation window 3/13–3/23 for both groups. Absolute volume metrics (total installs, total revenue) should be interpreted within this window; rate-normalized metrics (RPM, Imp-to-Install%) remain the most reliable signals.
⚡ Updated Primary Insight — CPI/RPI Now Fully Resolved (v2)
With updated Gumshoe data, the Test group now exposes its CPI field ($9.17), making a direct apples-to-apples comparison possible for the first time. The key finding: both CPI and RPI are lower in Test, but the RPI/CPI margin is nearly identical — 1.90x Test vs 1.88x Control (+1.1%). This resolves the previous ambiguity. The suppression logic drove more installs at lower cost-per-install, while delivering proportionally lower revenue-per-install (mix dilution from higher volume). The net effect on RPM is legitimately positive (+11%) and is now explained by the decomposition: RPM = RPI × I2I — Test's +40.3% I2I gain more than offsets its −20.9% RPI dilution, producing the observed +11% RPM lift. The RPM improvement is real efficiency, not a revenue-inflation artifact.
Remaining caveat: The client's early pausing of the control means the data window for both groups is 3/13–3/23. Zero creative overlap remains an unresolved confound — it does not materially change the per-install or per-impression efficiency story, but the creative quality gap is still an independent variable that contributed ~10–15% of the I2I lift separately from suppression logic.

Creative Diversity Effect

Test group's revenue was more distributed (top creative at 42.2% vs Control's 49.6%). A more balanced creative mix reduces audience fatigue and widens reach across user segments. This structural advantage is independent of suppression logic and likely contributed ~5–10% of the install lift.
Top Creative Quality Gap

Test's top creative (1349603) achieved I2I rate of 0.1484% vs Control's top (1339890) at 0.1009% — a 47% gap at the creative level alone. Even if impression volumes were equalized, Test's head creative would still outperform. This is a meaningful independent signal separate from the experiment logic.
D7 ROAS Data Truncation Risk

Control group has D7 ROAS data on only 1 of 8 creatives, likely because the 7-day attribution window was cut short when the campaign was terminated on 3/23. This prevents a full quality comparison on downstream events (purchase, activation). Test group has 3 creatives with D7 ROAS data.
Estimated Effect Attribution (Internal)

Of the +40.3% Imp-to-Install lift:
• Suppression logic: est. ~20–25%
• Creative quality difference: est. ~10–15%
• Creative diversity / mix: est. ~5%

Rough internal estimate only — not for client delivery without a controlled re-run.
05 Internal Summary & Recommended Framing
Conclusions & Internal Action Items
✅ Suppression directionally works: Both primary normalized metrics point the same direction — RPM +11%, Imp-to-Install +40.3%. The experiment hypothesis is supported, even with the current data limitations.
⚠️ RPM caveat for internal review: The +11% RPM lift is partially a product of the Test group's higher absolute revenue base (+29%), not purely an efficiency signal. When presenting to clients, lead with Imp-to-Install rate improvement as the cleaner normalized metric. RPM can be mentioned as a supporting data point with appropriate caveats.
🎨 Creative imbalance is the primary confound: Zero creative overlap, Test has 75% more creatives, and Test's top creative independently converts at 47% higher rate. We cannot cleanly attribute performance delta to suppression alone. This is a key internal caveat before making any bold claims about suppression effectiveness.
⏱️ Experiment execution failure: Client terminated control early. All absolute metrics are compromised. We should document this as a protocol violation and factor it into how confidently we present findings. Recommend a re-run with stricter adherence to parallel flight requirements.
📌 For clean results next time: (1) Lock both groups to same flight duration; (2) Align creative pools — either same creatives in both or a statistically matched set; (3) Extend observation window to capture D7/D30 CPA-purchase and CPA-activation data fully; (4) If possible, run at campaign group level to increase sample size and statistical power.
